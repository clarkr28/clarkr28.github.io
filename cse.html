Computer Science

Research

This fall (2018), I started doing research with a computer science group at MSU focused on the assurance of autonomous systems. While many research groups are focused on developing autonomous systems, our focus is on how to test these systems to learn more about the limits of their capabilities. By being able to disciver the limits of a system and how its performance degrades as it reaches these limits, developers of these systems will be better equipped to make them more robust and reliable. My work involves using ROS to program our robots, Gazebo to simulate them, and genetic algorithms do discover limitations of their capabilities.

Artificial Intelligence

Like most computer science students, I find AI to be very interesting - no surprise there. Quite honestly, it's one of the biggest reasons why I decided to pursue a master's degree. I have the privilege of taking dedicated courses in machine learning, computer vision, data science, deep learning, pattern recognition, and other fascinating topics that aren't under the AI umbrella. My research has me very interested in how robotics companies are using AI to develop more capable robots. I'm also interested in learning about how companies with large amounts of data are using artificial intelligence to their advantage.

ARM Programming

One of my undergraduate courses that I found most interesting was my computer architecture class. In that class, we learned about how computers actually work down to the hardware level. What made this class so interesting to me was that there was no more "magic" about how computers work. It also introduced me to programming in assembly - our class focused on the ARM architecture. Even though writing a program in assembly took me longer than writing the same program in C, I was facinated by the challenges that programming in assembly presented.
